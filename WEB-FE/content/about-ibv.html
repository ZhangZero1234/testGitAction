Who we are

Founded in 2001, the IBM Institute for Business Value, (IBV), represents IBM's substantial commitment to and investment in business thought leadership.

The IBM Institute for Business Value (IBV) provides senior executives with strategic insights and recommendations that address critical business challenges and capitalize on new opportunities, all with an eye on value. The IBV conducts industry, technology, and business role research and analysis. We work in collaboration with industry experts, leading edge clients, and our own consultants to provide practical recommendations built on a foundation of fundamental research.

To learn more, you may contact the IBV team.

 

What we do

Working with clients and IBM practitioners, the IBV provides thought leadership on critical challenges to help organizations realize business value. IBV research teams are aligned with the GBS priorities, industries and business, and develop thought leadership for the major process areas. The IBV conducts five types of research:

  • Industry topics

    These studies are in-depth assessments of today's critical issues, opportunities and value potential with strategic and tactical recommendations for next steps for a specific industry.

  • Business topics

    These studies identify the emerging trends and issues relevant to C-level executives as well as pinpoint and analyze important strategic shifts that, with IBM's help, allows clients to capitalize on the opportunities.

  • Technology topics

    Detailed studies of current trends, imperatives and competitive implications covering either a functional area such as procurement or emerging technology trends.

  • C-suite Study program

    The Global C-suite study is a bi-annual research project designed to explore the latest competitive trends and challenges across the world, viewed through the lens of C-suite executives. From that research, we publish a global report (cross CxO), six role reports (CEO, CFO, CHRO, CIO, CMO, COO) and as many as 20 industry perspectives. The objectives of the program are to:

    • Maintain IBM's position as a leader in C-suite level Strategy focused thought leadership
    • Grow and deepen CxO relationships across the world
    • Forecast future business trends to assist clients

  • Performance data and Benchmarking program

    IBM is an international leader in business process optimization. The IBV provides a process benchmarking service that helps clients measure their current state and compare their performance against peers.

The main thought leadership assets typically consist of:

  • Expert Insights (typically consist of 1,500 words, 1 figure)

    Expert insights represent the opinions of thought leaders on newsworthy business and related technology topics. They are based upon conversations with leading subject matter experts from around the globe.

  • Research Insights (typically consist of 3,500 words, 6-10 figures)

    Research insights are fact-based strategic insights for business executives on critical public and private sector issues. They are based on findings from analysis of our own primary research studies.

  • Benchmark Insights (can follow short or longer formats noted above)

    Benchmark insights feature insights for executives on important business and related technology topics. They are based on analysis of performance data and other benchmarking measures.

 

What the IBV can do for you

Along with each thought leadership report, the IBV produces a variety of accompanying assets such as client-ready presentation decks, social tiles, infographics, and more. These supplemental assets are available only to IBMers and are meant to fortify IBMer impact with clients, business partners and organizations.

IBV thought leadership materials can help you:

  • Understand the latest industry trends: Anticipate changes, identify best practices and map your client's position against the industry on a global scale.
  • Develop comprehensive client roadmaps: Formulate fact-based strategies and identify operational alternatives to help your client initiate change.
  • Increase sales: Strengthen your credibility in the sales cycle with industry points-of-view from the IBV and by tailoring IBV thought leadership materials to your client's needs.
  • Strengthen client relationships: Show IBM is investing in areas that matter to your client by presenting client-ready analysis from IBV studies or invite an IBV expert speaker to present a client executive briefing.
  • Build the brand: Publish articles and interviews in the local press based on thought leadership to reinforce the brand, and drive lead generation.
  • Quantify your client's returns: Determine the most effective measurement of strategic success to calculate your client's expected return on investment.
